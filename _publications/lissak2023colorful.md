<summary>Abstract</summary>
Queer youth face increased mental health risks, such as depression, anxiety, and suicidal ideation.
Hindered by negative stigma, they often avoid seeking help and rely on online resources, which may provide inaccurate information.
Although access to a supportive environment and reliable information is invaluable, many queer youth worldwide have no access to such support.
However, this could soon change due to the rapid adoption of Large Language Models (LLMs) such as ChatGPT.
This paper aims to harness the potential of LLMs to revolutionize queer emotional support and validation.
To this end, we conduct a qualitative and quantitative analysis of ChatGPT's interactions with queer-related content.
To evaluate response quality, we develop a ten-question scale that is inspired by psychological standards and expert input.
We apply this scale to score ChatGPT responses and human comments to Reddit posts where queer youth seek advice and share experiences.
We find that ChatGPT responses are supportive and inclusive, outscoring humans. However, they tend to be generic, not empathetic enough, and lack personalization, resulting in nonreliable and potentially harmful advice.
We then discuss the inherent challenges of assessing AI queer support and introduce a classification scheme focused on reliability, empathy, and personalization.
Finally, we propose our vision of a queer supporter that actively (and sensitively) seeks user context to provide tailored, empathetic, and reliable responses.
